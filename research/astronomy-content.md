# Astronomy Content Guidance

## Credible V1 Categories
- Nearby stars and the local stellar neighborhood
- Constellation anchors and sky-context overlays
- Nebulae, open clusters, and globular clusters
- Notable compact objects such as black hole candidates and pulsars
- Well-studied exoplanet systems
- Milky Way context layers and named galactic regions

## What Stays Curated
- Deep-space featured collections
- Milky Way framing layers
- Tour narratives and “interesting now” suggestions
- Most exoplanet and compact-object highlights

## What Can Be Refreshed
- Catalog metadata and descriptive notes
- Coordinates, distances, and identifiers when upstream releases change
- NASA image references and content metadata

## Honesty Rules
- Do not label deep-space catalog objects as live.
- Use labels such as `cataloged`, `refreshed`, `predicted`, or `observed`.
- Present provenance and update cadence as a trust cue, not as a defensive disclaimer.
- Reserve `live` for truly time-sensitive orbit and event domains.
